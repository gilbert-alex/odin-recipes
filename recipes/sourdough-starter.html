Sourdough Starter

A bubbly goop of yeast, water, and flour.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 2 cups warm water

Instructions:

  • Step 1

    • Place flour into a large, non-metallic bowl. Sprinkle yeast over top, pour in warm water, and mix to combine.

  • Step 2

    • Loosely cover the bowl and set in a warm place to ferment for 4 to 8 days. Depending on temperature and humidity, times may vary. The starter is ready when bubbly and pleasantly sour smelling. If it's colored at all (pink, orange, ect.), throw it out and start over.

Pro Tip

If you use any of the starter, replace with the same volume of flour and water. You can optionally toss in a pinch of sugar to increase fermentation. Mix until well combined and leave out on the counter until bubbly again before returning the mixture to the refrigerator.

A light brown liquid may accumulate on top of the mixture. If this occurs simply stir back into the starter before using.
